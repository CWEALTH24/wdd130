Nigeria is a country in West Africa known for its diverse culture, rich history, and significant oil reserves, it is also home to a large population and a vibrant economy. Nigeria's cultural diversity is reflected in its numerous ethnic groups, languages, and traditions.Nigeria stretches from the Sahel in the north to the Atlantic’s Gulf of Guinea in the south. Its diverse landscape includes plains, plateaus, and the fertile Niger–Benue river basin. The country is home to more than 250 ethnic groups and over 500 languages, with the Hausa–Fulani, Yoruba, and Igbo being the largest. Islam predominates in the north, Christianity in the south, and traditional religions persist across regions. Nigeria possesses abundant natural resources, particularly petroleum and natural gas, concentrated in the Niger Delta. Oil dominates exports and government revenue, though agriculture and services employ much of the population. Recent reforms aim to diversify the economy into manufacturing, digital technology, and renewable energy. Despite being Africa’s largest economy, challenges include income inequality, infrastructure deficits, and reliance on oil volatility.
